My Childhood

Hobbies

Polymer clay jewellery making

This type of clay was a hit during my childhood, because it could be hardened in a normal oven and it had many different colours.

Luckily I do not have any examples of the jewellery I made, because it was surely hideous.

Writing

Writing fiction - mostly about children having adventures. Features included an unlikely number of twins, complete absence of school and unnecessarily long descriptions of everyone's outfits.

My dad was a book editor at that stage and I was convinced I was going to write his next hit. The poor man dutifully read all the nonsense I wrote, never discouraging me. The hit, obviously, was not forthcoming.

Toys

Little Professor

The Little Professor was an electronic game where you did increasingly difficult mental arithmetic against time.

Thinking back now, I can not imagine a little kid being interested in this toy. I, however, spent so many hours with it that teachers had to give me other activities to do in class, while the other kids did maths.

Moviemaker boardgame

Moviemaker was a boardgame where you 'made' movies by combining playing cards for actors, directors, etc.

I can not remember how the game worked, just that you got fewer points when you combined cards from different genres in the same movie. I also recall that dramas were worth the most points and horror films the least - typical.
